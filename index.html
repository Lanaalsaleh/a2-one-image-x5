The Glitch Gallery

This is an image i took at The Art Gallery of Ontario transformed five times to explore the concepts of distortion, imperfection, and glitch aesthetics inspired by The Glitch Studies Manifesto.

scroll to explore the transformations.

Distorted Perfection

Inspired by The Glitch Studies Manifesto’s idea that technology is never completely perfect, this section uses scaling, contrast, rotation, translation, blur, and repetition to subtly distort the original image without fully destroying it. The image remains recognizable, reflecting how glitches expose imperfections rather than hide them.

Fregmented Systems

This transformation explores Menkman's idea that glitches expose the systems behind digital media. by fragmenting the image intro seprated pieces using CSS Grid, spacing, rotation, the image becomes unstable and visibly constructed rather than smooth and complete.

Glitch Aesthetics

This transformation explores glitch aesthetics through layering, color distortion,opacity, and visual interference. multiple versions of the same image overlap and seprate, creating an unsettled visual experince that reflects how glitches interrupt the illusion of perfect digital media and expose technological instability.

Corrupted signal

Corrupted signal uses grayscale, blur, distortion, and layered interference to interrupt the readability of the original image. instead of presenting a clear and stable photograph, the image becomes partially unreadable and visually disrupted, reflecting the Glitch Studies Manifesto's idea that glitches interrupt normal systems of communication and perception
